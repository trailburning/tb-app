Running wild in the
South Australian bush

by Matt Allbeury   08/04/2014

It was just after dawn and there was a heavy mist covering the hills as we pushed deeper into the bush, but then our run became something unexpected as we glimpsed our first kangaroo through the mist, and then another, and another. We were running through a herd of wild kangaroo and our hearts raced, it was something almost primeval. There we were running amongst wild animals with a sense that we too were wild. At that moment I felt I understood what it means to run - itâ€™s not about speed, distance or the finish line; to run is to run wild, as wild as an animal.

I was running with Paul Tran and it was our first serious trail run in the Adelaide Hills. Both of us had spent many years in the shadows of the hills, Paul as a native Adelaidean and myself as a long term resident, and yet neither of us had spent a great deal of time in them. We researched a half marathon route and started at the base of the hills in the suburb of Burnside, climbing up to just over 700 metres to Mt Lofty before heading back via Chambers Gully, looping back to our start point down in the suburbs.

This run was to affect both of us quite profoundly. Paul has since gone on to competitive finishes in both the Yurrebilla 56 km and Heysen 105 km trail events. He can be found at dawn every Thursday in the Adelaide Hills, running with a bunch of likeminded individuals.

Myself, well for me this is where Trailburning started. I felt that such was the experience of running wild, running purely for the enjoyment of it, that there should be a platform to share such experiences. What if there was a way for anyone to share their trail, inspiring others to discover the hidden treasures of their own backyard, what if?
